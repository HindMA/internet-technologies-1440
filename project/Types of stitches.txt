<h2> Types of stitches </h2>
<p><b>There are five main types of basic stitches (the following description uses US crochet terminology which differs from the terminology used in the UK and Europe).</b></p>

<ol>
 <li> Chain Stitch – the most basic of all stitches and used to begin most projects.</li>
 <li> Slip Stitch – used to join chain stitch to form a ring.</li>
 <li> Single Crochet Stitch (called Double Crochet Stitch in the UK) – easiest stitch to master<a href="https://www.thesprucecrafts.com/single-crochet-stitch-tutorial-979083"> Single Crochet Stitch Tutorial</a></li>
 <li> Half Double Crochet Stitch (called Half Treble Stitch in the UK) – the 'in-between' stitch <a href="https://www.thesprucecrafts.com/how-to-half-double-crochet-stitch-3576850"> Half-Double Crochet Tutorial</a></li>
 <li> Double Crochet Stitch (called Treble Stitch in the UK) – many uses for this unlimited use stitch <a href="https://www.thesprucecrafts.com/double-crochet-979081">Double Crochet Stitch Tutorial</a></li>
</ol>

<p>While the horizontal distance covered by these basic stitches is the same, they differ in height and thickness.<br><br><b>
<div class="c"> The more advanced stitches are often combinations of these basic stitches, or are made by inserting the hook into the work in unusual locations. More advanced stitches include the Shell Stitch, V Stitch, Spike 
 <div class="c">Stitch, Afghan Stitch, Butterfly Stitch, Popcorn Stitch, Cluster stitch, and Crocodile Stitch.</b></p>
</div></div>
